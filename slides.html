What is Semantic HTML?

Using HTML tags that describe the meaning and purpose of the content.

  • Crucial for Accessibility (A11y): Helps screen readers understand the page structure.
  • Crucial for SEO: Helps search engines understand your content.

Core Semantic Tags: Part 1

<header>

Introductory content for a page or section (logos, title, navigation).

<nav>

Specifically for main navigation links.

Core Semantic Tags: Part 2

<main>

The primary, unique content of the page. (Only one per page!)

<section>

A thematic grouping of content, usually with a heading.

Core Semantic Tags: Part 3

<article>

Self-contained content that could stand on its own (e.g., a blog post, a project card).

<footer>

Closing content for a page or section (copyright, contact info).

Example: Portfolio Structure


<body>
  <header>
    <nav>...</nav>
  </header>

  <main>
    <section id="about">
      <h1>About Me</h1>
    </section>

    <section id="projects">
      <h2>My Projects</h2>
      <article>...</article>
      <article>...</article>
    </section>
  </main>

  <footer>
    <p>© 2025 Your Name</p>
  </footer>
</body>

Key Takeaway

Always choose the HTML tag that best describes the meaning of the content.
